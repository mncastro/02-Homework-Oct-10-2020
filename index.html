About Me

Bilingual translator, interpreter and researcher with higher education degrees from Mexico and the United States. My broad interests have led me to explore numerous subjects and areas of knowledge, as well as gain experience in numerous jobs in different fields and transnational environments. I am looking for unique and demanding jobs with flexible scheduling that require improvisation and creativity. In the last ten years I have worked in both Mexico and the USA as a creative consultant, translator, proofreader, data analyst, and teacher in numerous fields. I have published academic articles and book reviews, and developed interdisciplinary research projects combining literary criticism, film analysis, anthropology, digital tools, history and geography. I'm currently a doctoral candidate in the PhD program in Latin American Studies at the Teresa Lozano Long Institute of Latin American Studies in The University of Texas at Austin. Because of my doctoral research, I'll travel through Mexico and the US in the next two years and I'm excited to explore new types of jobs and share my skills with institutions, business companies and nonprofit associations.
